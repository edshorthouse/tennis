🏆 Ultimate World Champion

A fictional lineal tennis title, boxing-style: held until you lose.

The Ultimate World Champion is a fictional lineal tennis title. The first holder is the winner of the first Open Era match — Owen Davidson, on 22 April 1968 — and the title then passes only when the reigning champion loses a match. It is tracked over Jeff Sackmann's ATP match data, filtered to consistent tour-level events (Grand Slams, Masters, ATP 500/250 and the Tour Finals).

Strict lineal rule: there is no vacancy clause. If a champion retires without losing, the title stays dormant with them — which is why the current holder is a player from an earlier era.
